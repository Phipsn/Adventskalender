The Mats & Roxy Riddle Advent Calendar

Advent riddle fun for the whole family with Mats and Roxy

Participate in the Riddle Advent Calendar, get to know Mats and Roxy in charming short stories, win a real gold treasure, and support a good cause!

Instead of chocolate, three new riddles and short stories are released each week. The riddles are published for everyone at the same time and build on each other. The person who solves the last riddle on the 4th Advent, December 22, 2024, first, will win a real gold bar!

Only the last riddle on the 4th Advent determines who solves it first. For all the previous riddles, speed doesn't matter – you have time until the next riddle is published. All riddles, characters, ideas, and short stories come from my own imagination – not a single sentence was written by ChatGPT or similar tools.

What to expect:
Solving riddles, unwinding, puzzling together, reading short stories, and perhaps reading to someone to bring joy – and supporting a good cause at the same time: That's the Mats & Roxy Riddle Advent Calendar!

Riddles and Process

The riddles are wrapped in charming short stories by Mats the raccoon and Roxy the fox. The stories often contain hints and tips "between the lines", but the task is always clearly stated. Mostly, they are not general knowledge questions (e.g., "What is the capital of xyz?") or classic puzzles. It is more about combining and figuring out how a hint might be interpreted. It’s not about knowledge but about thinking and researching.

The riddles come with short stories (about 2 A4 pages) from Mats the raccoon and Roxy the fox. The stories are designed like a children's book. If you wish, you can receive additional stories without puzzles from December 1-24.

You can answer until the next riddle is published. On Saturdays, you have one day, otherwise several days. It usually doesn’t take longer than a few minutes to solve a riddle.

You can still participate and solve the other riddles, but you will no longer be eligible for the prizes. EXCEPTION: It’s possible that you aren’t the only one who couldn’t solve a riddle. If everyone has struggled with the same riddle, the person with the most correct answers wins – so you could still win. Keep solving!

The riddles are published for everyone simultaneously via WhatsApp or email. Speed matters only for the last riddle.
